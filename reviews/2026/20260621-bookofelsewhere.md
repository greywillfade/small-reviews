This one fluctuated between a 2.5 at the start and a 3 by the end but could have gone either way for me. I'm familiar with but haven't read the BRZRKR series, and maybe I'd have got more out of it if I had, but overall this just left me a bit cold. There were some interesting parts, particularly from the perspective of various companions who'd spent time with the main character over the years, but overall the story felt pretty weak and a bit disjointed. Too many threads, but not enough of a strong core to bring them all back together. It does however get bonus points for what I could only interpret as a 'sad keanu' meme reference at the start of the book where the main character (who is obviously played by Keanu) sits on a bench with his elbows on his knees.
