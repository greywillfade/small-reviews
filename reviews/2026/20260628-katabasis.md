A chunky read which I got through pretty quickly thanks to some hot days indoors and pregnancy-related insomnia. 

I really liked the world building and the different details when describing Hell (whilst also leaving some of it still to interpretation), but whilst it was apt for the characters I wasn't fully on board with all of the intellectualising, philosophising, and mathematising. The two (three?) main characters were nicely fleshed out, and I did enjoy the balance of the present time being interspersed with flashbacks to learn more about them. Obviously they (on the most part) are incredibly flawed, and there were some slightly uncomfortable but very real parts of the story around power, coercion, misogyny, and self worth. I've thankfully not been deep in academia enough to directly relate to a lot of it in that setting, but in terms of other settings like working in tech it's still apt.

Ultimately despite some of the heavier themes it was still a fun, engaging, and creative read, and definitely helped me get through some long nights without ever feeling like a slog!
