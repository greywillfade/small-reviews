I went straight into the sequel (thanks also to my husband for the free book) and immediately enjoyed being back in the world. The main characters move to a new area and we see a bit more of their personalities and complexities emerge, plus get to find out some more world building about the Empire and all the bioscience stuff that goes on. In terms of the mystery it's kind of similar to the first book – starting off with a new tricky murder to figure out, which rapidly expands into a much more complex case. Throughout there are again some reveals and new developments that help you feel like you're making progress, although I would say that I found this plot slightly more predictable in terms of big moments. The ending in particular I had suspected was coming, but it still all played out in quite a satisfying way. 

I was so pleased to find out that there's a third book serendipitously coming out soon so I don't have to wait and forget everything (this seems to keep happening, yay), so even though it'll be in hardback (boo) I'm looking forward to getting stuck into that too.
