I still have regrets from watching that one series of the TV show, and I feel like I'm going to moan about it polluting my character imagination forever now. Anyway. The book.

I hadn't Murderbotted since September last year, so again obviously I've forgotten some details of what happened in the last book, but actually it wasn't too bad this time. This one has some key crossovers with previous other important characters and plot points though, so note to future me that it's worth keeping that in mind.

Despite that though, this is a pretty standalone extraction-based scenario. It didn't grab me or feel too unique at first, as we've already had 'Murderbot does extraction', and at a couple of points I found myself thinking "I'm still really enjoying reading this and I love Murderbot as much as ever... but would it be better to have fewer, stronger stories, or a clear end point?". The answer is probably no, as long as there are more I will keep reading them because I'm a sucker for this stuff, but equally this definitely isn't the strongest book. The stakes do get upped towards the end, and there are as always some pretty cool set pieces, plus we get to know even _more_ of the overall cast of people in SecUnit's life. But definitely not up there in the top tier.
